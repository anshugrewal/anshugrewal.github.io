--- layout: default title: Basics of Data Structures and Algorithms description: A beginner-friendly article on DSA concepts. --- # 🧠 Basics of Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) **By Anshu Grewal** | **Posted on April 14, 2025** --- ## 📘 What is DSA? **DSA (Data Structures and Algorithms)** is the foundation of computer science — used to store, organize, and process data efficiently. ### 🧱 Data Structures (DS) These are ways to organize data: - **Array** – Fixed-size, index-based collection - **Linked List** – Nodes connected via pointers - **Stack** – Last-In-First-Out (LIFO) - **Queue** – First-In-First-Out (FIFO) - **Hash Table** – Key-value storage - **Tree** – Hierarchical structure - **Graph** – Nodes and edges, perfect for networks --- ### ⚙️ Algorithms (A) Algorithms are step-by-step instructions to solve problems: - **Sorting** – Bubble, Merge, Quick Sort - **Searching** – Linear, Binary Search - **Recursion** – When a function calls itself - **Greedy, Dynamic Programming, Backtracking** – Advanced strategies --- ## 💡 Why Learn DSA? - Improves **problem-solving** ability - Helps write **efficient** and **scalable** code - It's essential for **technical interviews** and competitive programming --- ## 🚀 What’s Next?
